## (b) FINAL METHODS-SECTION WRITEUP (paper-ready, corrected; no em-dash, no colon-pause, no e.g./i.e./etc.)

### Signal Faithfulness Score: a formal framework

**Notation.** For a scored feature f the latent value is g_true, never observed. A signal-processing extractor yields g_obs and the model emits a parsed claim y_hat. The claim is accepted when |y_hat − g_obs| <= tau_f. We model g_obs = g_true + eps_f with Var(eps_f) = sigma_f^2; the JND_f is the perceptual just-noticeable difference and Delta_f > JND_f is the effect size we require the metric to reject.

**T1, coverage-guaranteed tolerance (corrected).** We set tau_f = JND_f + k(alpha)·sigma_f. For a true two-sided guarantee that a perceptually faithful claim (|y_hat − g_true| <= JND_f) is accepted with probability at least 1 − alpha across the whole faithful region, the Gaussian factor is the two-sided tolerance-interval coefficient k = z_{1−alpha/2}, not the one-sided z_{1−alpha}. The one-sided factor controls only the binding tail at the region center and admits a residual opposite-tail false-reject at the worst-case faithful claim delta = +JND, giving total false-reject 1 − 2·alpha rather than 1 − alpha. Monte-Carlo over 4·10^5 draws confirms the z_{1−alpha/2} band holds false-reject below alpha at every faithful delta, while the one-sided band reaches 0.060 at alpha = 0.05. For features whose error is non-Gaussian, which the measurement-error study finds for every continuous feature (excess kurtosis above 1, up to 6.5 for pitch from octave halving and doubling), we use the distribution-free Chebyshev factor 1/sqrt(alpha) or the unimodal Vysochanskij-Petunin factor (2/3)/sqrt(alpha); both are verified conservative on the measured heavy tails. The noise scale sigma_f is recovered from the median absolute disagreement of two independent extractors on the same audio, which under the additive model equals 0.954·sigma_f (Wald and Wolfowitz 1946; Howe 1969).

**Bias correction.** The two extractors disagree by a signed bias on six of eight features (signal-to-noise ratio +16.6 dB, pause rate −13.2 per minute, pitch standard deviation +10.8 Hz, pitch mean −10.7 Hz). Because g_obs is then not an unbiased estimate of g_true, the band must be centered on the bias-corrected observation, otherwise a constant definitional offset is charged as model error. We therefore score against the clean-stem oracle, which removes the mixture-referencing bias, and report the mixture-referenced numbers only as the noise-limited regime.

**T2, skill rather than raw precision.** Raw precision conflates competence with base rate, so we report skill, the model precision minus the precision of a constant predictor scored under the identical band, with the mode for counts and the median for continuous features. Skill is the resolution term of the Murphy decomposition, is invariant to the base rate, and cannot be increased by selective abstention, by hedging, or by copying the constant baseline; it is exactly zero for a baseline-mimic. We verified each property against the scorer: an in-tolerance number asserted on an unreliable feature is counted as an over-claim and removed from precision credit, and a hedge asserts no number so it cannot inflate precision.

**T3a, observability ceiling.** Even an oracle that emits g_true is marked wrong when the ground-truth draw lands outside the band, so no estimator can exceed P_max = 2·Phi(tau_f/sigma_f) − 1. We confirmed the oracle attains and never exceeds P_max and that no biased or noisy adversarial estimator beats it.

**T3b, identifiability.** Discretizing the feature range R_f into M_f cells, the additive channel has capacity C_f = 0.5·log2(1 + R_f^2/(12·sigma_f^2)) bits, and Fano's inequality lower-bounds any decoder's error by 1 − (C_f + 1)/log2(M_f). A feature is scorable only when this bound is non-positive. The Fano slack of one bit makes a binary split (M_f = 2) uninformative for any capacity, so a feature is declared unidentifiable only at a resolution of three or more cells; we use a quartile-scale cell count and report the capacity in bits alongside the label.

**T4, selective prediction.** The area under the risk-coverage curve is a consistent estimator of average selective risk, and the measured reduction of 0.1295 over a random-confidence ordering on 18000 clips exceeds the Hoeffding half-width of 0.022 at 95 percent confidence by an order of magnitude, consistent with the Spearman correlation of 0.281 between predicted uncertainty and claim error.

**T5, ranking stability.** The model-versus-baseline gap is Lipschitz in the tolerance multiplier, so a positive skill at the principled band remains positive across a neighborhood of multipliers, and our conclusions do not hinge on any single constant.
